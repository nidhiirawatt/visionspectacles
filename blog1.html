But first, what exactly is the anti reflective coating?

The anti-glare glasses contain lenses with the upper coating of the anti-reflection on either side, which reduces the glare significantly. This elimination of glare or reflection helps you see clearly as it lets lighter pass through the lens and reach your eye without harming it. In addition, this anti-glare coating keeps your eyes comfortable and keeps the delicate skin around the eyes safe while reducing eye strain.

Benefits of Anti Reflective Coatings on Eyeglasses

Lens Lifespan:
The anti reflective coating also keeps your lens scratch-free, prolonging its life. This will help you save money as you will not have to replace your lens often. In addition, anti-glare coatings provide an extra layer of safety to glass lenses from either side, which is why people opt for them.
Protecting Your Eyes from Harmful Rays
Ultraviolet rays can damage your eyes significantly over time, and taking proper precautions is advised, especially if you struggle with eye ailments. The anti reflective coating eliminates all the reflection from the lens surface and stops it from reaching your eyes, defending it from harmful UV rays. These reflections mostly come from the windows and sidewalks. You can add an anti reflective coating on the inner side of your lenses, which will fulfill the purpose.

Different Lenses with Anti Reflective Coatings to Choose From.

For those still unaware of their options, here are the diverse types of lenses with anti-reflective or anti-glare coating.

Progressive lenses:
The bifocal lenses allow you to focus on close objects and objects in the distance. You might see your grandparents wearing these lenses.
Transition lenses:
These lenses adjust according to light in the surrounding. These are primarily used in sunglasses but are also making a comeback in eyeglasses.
Standard single vision:
The most common lens type, this single standard vision, is used in glasses for people who have shortsightedness or farsightedness.
Blue Light lens:
These light filters all the blue light radiated from electronic devices, keeping your eyes safe from extreme stress due to harmful rays.
